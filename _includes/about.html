What we do

Our mission is to empower use of the City’s data. Our core product is Raleigh Open Data Portal, our official open data portal. Launched in 2009, the data portal contains hundreds of city datasets for use by developers, analysts, residents, and more. We believe open data has the potential to support a range of outcomes from increased quality of life, more efficient government services, better decisions, and new businesses and services.

We’ve also learned that enabling data use in Raleigh is about more than simply publishing datasets on Raleigh OpenData. That’s why we’ve launched programs like the Data Academy, a training program which helps increase internal City capacity to effectively use our data.

And we’re extending the open data approach beyond the simple publication of datasets to using open data as a means to reveal and unpack complex areas such as affordable housing, homelessness, and economic development. Many areas of government service touch multiple departments through multiple programs. Our approach is to identify the various programs and their administrative data and then publish that data along with the program context. We call these strategic releases and our theory is that this will yield quick value from open datasets that may otherwise be released disparately and without broader context.
